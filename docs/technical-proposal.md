2.	INTRODUCTION
2.1.	Background
Adolescents and youth represent a significant proportion of the population and are at a critical stage of development where access to accurate, timely, and youth-friendly Sexual and Reproductive Health and Rights (SRHR) information is essential. However, many young people continue to face substantial barriers in accessing reliable SRHR information and services. These barriers include stigma, restrictive cultural and social norms, limited confidentiality within health systems, and inadequate access to trusted and youth-responsive platforms.
As a result, adolescents and youth remain disproportionately affected by poor SRHR outcomes, including unintended pregnancies, sexually transmitted infections (STIs), and gender-based violence (GBV). Misinformation, fear of judgment, and concerns about privacy further discourage young people from seeking information and utilizing available health services, particularly in contexts where youth-friendly services are limited or not easily accessible.
At the same time, the rapid expansion of mobile technology and digital connectivity presents a transformative opportunity to address these challenges. Mobile applications offer a private, accessible, and scalable platform through which adolescents and youth can access accurate SRHR information, build knowledge, and make informed decisions about their health and well-being. Leveraging digital solutions is therefore increasingly recognized as a critical strategy for improving health literacy and service uptake among young people.
2.2.	 Rationale for the Consultancy
Despite the growing potential of digital health interventions, there remains a gap in comprehensive, user-centered, and interactive SRHR mobile platforms tailored to the diverse needs of adolescents and youth. Many existing solutions lack interactivity, inclusivity, cultural relevance, and sustainable content management systems, limiting their effectiveness, usability, and long-term impact.
Addressing this gap requires the development of an innovative, engaging, and evidence-based mobile application that goes beyond static information provision to actively promote positive behaviors, strengthen linkages to youth-friendly services, and provide a safe and confidential space for adolescents and youth to explore SRHR topics.
A key requirement of this consultancy is the application of user-centered design principles, ensuring that the app is informed by the real needs, preferences, and experiences of adolescents and youth. This includes conducting a needs assessment, engaging users throughout the design and testing process, and ensuring the final product is accessible, inclusive, and responsive to diverse groups, including young women, young men, adolescents, and marginalized populations.
Parker Dunne Consultants is pleased to submit this technical proposal to undertake this assignment. PDC brings extensive experience in SRHR programming, digital health solutions, public health research, and user-centered design, with a strong track record of delivering high-quality, evidence-based, and impactful interventions.
The proposed consultancy is designed to deliver a functional, user-friendly, and sustainable mobile application, supported by a robust content management system and informed by rigorous user testing and quality assurance processes. The application will provide adolescents and youth with improved access to accurate SRHR information, enhance their decision-making capacity, and strengthen linkages to available health services.
Ultimately, this initiative will contribute to strengthening youth-responsive health systems, improving SRHR outcomes, and empowering adolescents and youth with the knowledge and tools they need to lead healthy and informed lives.

PDC proposes a user-centered, iterative, and participatory methodology for the development of an interactive mobile application on SRHR for adolescents and youth. This methodology is designed to ensure:
i.	Strong alignment with adolescents’ and youths’ needs and preferences

5.1.	 Methodological Approach
The consultancy will adopt a four-phase iterative approach, integrating research, design, development, and validation:
5.1.1.	Human-Centered Design (HCD) Approach
The HCD approach will ensure that adolescents and youth are at the center of the entire process. 
i.	Understanding user needs, behaviors, and preferences: This will involve conducting a comprehensive needs assessment using both qualitative and quantitative approaches (e.g., surveys, focus group discussions, and user interviews) to explore adolescents’ and youths’ SRHR information needs, digital habits, content preferences, and barriers to accessing services. Special attention will be given to differences across age groups, gender, literacy levels, and vulnerable populations to ensure the application is relevant, inclusive, and contextually appropriate.
ii.	Co-creating solutions with target users: Adolescents and youth will be actively engaged throughout the design process to ensure the application reflects their realities, language, and expectations. This will include participatory design workshops, concept testing sessions, and feedback engagements where users contribute to defining features, content structure, user interface preferences, and functionality. This approach ensures ownership, acceptability, and alignment with user expectations.
iii.	Iterative testing and refinement: The application will undergo multiple cycles of prototyping, usability testing, and refinement. Early versions (wireframes and prototypes) will be tested with representative users to assess usability, navigation, comprehension, and engagement. Feedback will be systematically documented and incorporated into subsequent versions, ensuring continuous improvement. This iterative process will enhance user experience, functionality, and overall effectiveness prior to final deployment.

Measures will be put in place to protect user privacy, such as anonymous access to information, secure data handling, and discreet user interfaces. 5.3.	 Needs Assessment and User Research
A comprehensive needs assessment will be conducted to identify gaps in SRHR knowledge, service access, and digital engagement preferences among adolescents and youth.
Data Collection Methods
i.	Desk Review: A desk review will be conducted to analyze relevant documents, including national SRHR policies and strategies, existing digital health platforms, and studies on adolescent and youth SRHR. This will provide a strong contextual foundation and inform the overall design, content, and functionality of the application.
ii.	Key Informant Interviews (KIIs): Key informant interviews will be conducted with SRHR experts, healthcare providers, and program implementers to gather expert insights on existing gaps, service delivery challenges, and opportunities for digital innovation in SRHR programming for adolescents and youth.
iii.	Focus Group Discussions (FGDs): Focus group discussions will be conducted with adolescents (both in-school and out-of-school) and youth groups (male and female) to capture diverse perspectives and lived experiences. These discussions will explore SRHR knowledge gaps, information-seeking behaviors, preferences for digital platforms, and barriers to accessing SRHR services.
iv.	User Journey Mapping: User journey mapping will be applied to understand how adolescents and youth seek information, make decisions regarding SRHR, and interact with digital tools. This will help identify key touchpoints, challenges, and opportunities to enhance user experience and engagement within the application.
5.4.	App Design and Development
5.4.1.	Concept Development and Prototyping
This includes the development of the app architecture and features, the creation of wireframes and prototypes, and the validation of design concepts with end users to ensure usability, relevance, and alignment with user needs.
5.4.2.	Content Development
This involves the development of accurate, age-appropriate Sexual and Reproductive Health and Rights (SRHR) content that is tailored to the informational needs and comprehension levels of adolescents and young people. It also includes the integration of engaging multimedia formats such as text, audio, and visual materials to enhance understanding, accessibility, and user engagement across diverse literacy levels and learning preferences. In addition, all content will be carefully aligned with national SRHR guidelines and relevant global standards to ensure accuracy, credibility, cultural appropriateness, and consistency with best practices in youth-friendly health programming.
5.4.3.	Application Development
This includes the development of a fully functional mobile application, compatible with Android, iOS, or as a hybrid platform, ensuring broad accessibility for adolescents and young people across different devices. It also involves the integration of interactive features such as quizzes, frequently asked questions (FAQs), and a service locator to enhance user engagement, provide real-time information, and support easy navigation of available SRHR services. In addition, the assignment will include the development of a robust backend Content Management System (CMS) to enable efficient content updates, management, and scalability, ensuring that SRHR information remains current, accurate, and responsive to emerging needs.
5.4.4.	Key Features of the Application
The app will include:
i.	SRHR information modules: Structured sections within the app that provide clear, accurate, and age-appropriate information on key Sexual and Reproductive Health and Rights topics such as puberty, contraception, STI prevention, consent, and healthy relationships. 
ii.	Interactive learning tools (quizzes, scenarios): Engaging features that allow users to test their knowledge and apply learning through quizzes, case scenarios, and decision-making exercises that promote better understanding of SRHR concepts. 
iii.	Anonymous question-and-answer functionality: A safe and confidential feature that enables young people to ask SRHR-related questions without revealing their identity and receive reliable, youth-friendly responses from trained moderators or health experts. 
iv.	Linkages to youth-friendly health services: A referral and information system that connects users to nearby or relevant youth-friendly health facilities and services, helping them access counseling, contraception, testing, and other SRHR services. 
v.	Push notifications for health messaging: Automated alerts sent to users to share timely SRHR information, reminders, tips, and campaign messages to reinforce learning and encourage positive health behaviors.
5.5.	 User Testing and Validation

i. It verifies that all features such as interactive tools, navigation functions, and multimedia content, work effectively across Android, iOS, and different connectivity environments, including low-bandwidth settings. 
iii.	Content validation with SRHR experts: This process involves reviewing all SRHR content with technical experts to ensure accuracy, relevance, and alignment with national SRHR guidelines and global best practices. Experts assess the appropriateness of messaging, factual correctness, and cultural sensitivity to ensure the information is safe, reliable, and suitable for adolescent and youth audiences.
Feedback Integration
i.	Iterative refinement based on user feedback: This involves continuously improving the application through multiple cycles of development, where feedback collected from adolescents and youth during testing is used to adjust content, design, and functionality to better meet user needs and expectations. 
ii.	Identification and resolution of technical issues: This includes systematically detecting, documenting, and fixing bugs, errors, or performance challenges within the application to ensure it runs smoothly, reliably, and without disruptions across all supported devices. 
iii.	Enhancement of user experience and engagement: This focuses on improving the overall look, feel, and interactivity of the application by optimizing navigation, refining visual design, and strengthening interactive features to ensure the platform remains engaging, user-friendly, and appealing to adolescents and young people.
